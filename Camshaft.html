Camshaft

Camshafts have small lobes on them. The number of lobes a camshaft has is directly related to the number of valves and pistons an engine has. As the camshaft is rotated (1/2 the speed of the crankshaft) these lobes push on the valves or linkage connecting to the valves. This force causes the valves to open at precisely the correct time. Every engine has at least one camshaft. Some have as many as 4 or more depending on configuration.
